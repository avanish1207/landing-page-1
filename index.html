Whales are massive creatures that roam the ocean. They communicate with sounds and migrate across great distances.
Some random information.
Whales use sonar to emit sound waves that bounce back, helping them navigate and hunt.
Whales have a streamlined skeleton with reduced hind limbs, adapted for efficient swimming.
Whales have a unique anatomy with a large, robust body, blubber for insulation, and specialized organs for deep diving.
Whales share a common mammalian ancestry with humans, offering insights into evolution and marine ecosystems.
